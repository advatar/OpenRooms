OpenRooms (aka “StayMesh”): the open booking network

One-liner

An open, federated booking network where hotels keep the relationship, guests see the true total price, and fees are capped and transparent—so value flows to travelers and local businesses, not a central toll booth.

The problem (today)
	•	High, opaque fees: centralized OTAs tax each booking and hide the impact.
	•	Lock-in tactics: parity clauses and ranking pay-to-play extract margin from hotels.
	•	Data silos: reviews, identities, and listings are stuck inside one platform.
	•	Misaligned incentives: OTAs optimize ad spend and take rate—not guest experience or local ecosystems.

The solution (how it works)
	•	Open rails, many apps: A public Search & Offer API that any app can use. Hotels (or their PMS/channel managers) publish content, prices, and availability once; any front-end can consume it.
	•	Attribution without lock-in: Short-lived Booking-Intent Tokens (BITs) pay a small, capped lead fee when a discovery app sends a guest to book—solves “free-rider” fights without price-parity.
	•	Direct, safer payments: Escrow splits funds automatically (hotel + tiny lead fee). Release on check-in; refunds follow clear policies.
	•	Portable, proof-backed reviews: Reviews are signed with a proof-of-stay receipt so they’re real—and portable across apps.
	•	Radical transparency: A public transparency log records fee caps, any paid placement, and ranking rules.

Why it’s better (everyone wins—except rent-seekers)

For travelers
	•	Lower total prices (fees capped at ~0.5–2%).
	•	Clear cancellation rules up front.
	•	Reviews you can trust (proof-of-stay), portable across apps.
	•	Privacy by design—share only what’s needed to book/check-in.

For hotels & small groups
	•	Keep the customer relationship and your brand.
	•	No price-parity clauses; be cheaper on your own site if you wish.
	•	Predictable, minimal distribution cost; attribution that actually works.
	•	Direct payouts on check-in; fewer chargebacks via escrow.

For local ecosystems (DMOs, associations, PMS vendors)
	•	Run federation gateways that onboard many properties at once.
	•	Lower barriers for niche and regional apps (sport, wellness, eco stays).
	•	Open standards reduce costly one-off integrations.

How it compares (at a glance)
	•	Centralized OTA: closed inventory, ranking paywalls, 12–25% take rate, locked reviews.
	•	OpenRooms: open inventory rails, transparent ranking, ~1% infra + optional small lead fee, portable reviews & identity.

Business & governance
	•	Non-profit foundation + co-op governance (associations, PMSs, consumer apps).
	•	Revenue: tiny infra fee on escrow + optional membership; no ads-as-ranking.
	•	Rules: fee cap, no parity clauses, portability required, ranking disclosure.

Why now
	•	Hotels are pushing back on parity and dominance.
	•	PSD2/Open Banking + modern wallets make direct, safe payments trivial.
	•	Open standards (content, offers, identity) finally exist and are developer-friendly.

Go-to-market (12-month arc)
	1.	Pilot with one national association + 2 PMSs (50–100 hotels).
	2.	Consumer app + “Book on hotel site” widgets using BITs.
	3.	Proof-of-stay reviews unlock trusted reputation across the network.
	4.	Add verticals (boutique, eco, conferences) + regional gateways.

The promise

A fair marketplace where great stays surface because they’re great—
not because someone paid the most to stand between guests and hotels.
